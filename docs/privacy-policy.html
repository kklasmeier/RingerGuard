RingerGuard Privacy Policy

Effective date: July 22, 2026

RingerGuard ("the app") is developed by Kevin Klasmeier. This policy explains what the app does, what permissions it uses, and how your information is handled.

Summary

  • RingerGuard does not collect, sell, or share your personal data.
  • RingerGuard does not use analytics, advertising, or crash-reporting SDKs.
  • RingerGuard does not connect to the internet for its core features.
  • All settings and event logs stay on your device.

What the app does

RingerGuard monitors your phone's ringer and related audio settings. If your phone is unintentionally silenced, the app can restore your ringer after a grace period you configure, unless you explicitly chose to silence it through the app or widget.

Data stored on your device

The app stores the following locally on your phone only:

  • Your app settings (grace period, quiet hours, restore options, etc.)
  • An optional on-device event log (for example, when the ringer was restored)
  • Service timing metadata needed to run background checks

This data is not transmitted off your device by RingerGuard.

Permissions

RingerGuard may request the following permissions to provide its features:

  • Modify audio settings — restore ringer volume and ringer mode.
  • Do Not Disturb access — optionally turn off DND when restoring the ringer (if you enable this setting).
  • Foreground service — keep the protection service running reliably in the background.
  • Exact alarms — schedule reliable periodic checks, including in battery saver where allowed.
  • Boot completed — restart protection after your phone reboots.
  • Ignore battery optimizations — recommended for reliable operation on some devices (especially Samsung).
  • Phone state (optional) — only if you enable grace period on call dismiss. The app detects call-state changes only. It does not read your phone number, contacts, or call history.

What we do not access

  • Contacts
  • SMS or MMS messages
  • Call logs
  • Location
  • Photos, media, or files outside the app's own storage
  • Microphone or call audio

Children

RingerGuard is not directed at children under 13.

Data deletion

Uninstalling RingerGuard removes the app and its local data from your device. You can also clear the event log from within the app at any time.

Changes to this policy

This policy may be updated when the app changes. The effective date at the top of this page will be revised when updates are published.

Contact

Questions about this policy can be sent via the RingerGuard GitHub repository or through the developer contact listed on the app's Google Play Store listing.
